Analysis Plan Commitment

One of the most important steps we take is committing to a detailed analysis plan before we begin working with data. We commit ourselves to specific outcome variables and analytic methods, and we date-stamp the plan and post it on our website so that others can hold us accountable. With this, policymakers can base decisions on our results with confidence and researchers can verify that our methods are sound.

Reanalysis

All evaluations go through an internal replication. An independent reanalyst — an analyst who does not know the initial results — writes new code to analyze the data, working only from the raw data and the Analysis Plan. Any discrepancies between the two independent analyses are then addressed before the results are finalized.
